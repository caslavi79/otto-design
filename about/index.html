The Beginning

A house in Comfort, Texas.

In 2001, our grandfather Johnnie Otto Kveton invited us out to his land in Comfort, Texas to help him build his house. Not hire someone. Build it. Nail by nail, board by board, no waste, done by supper.

He didn't teach us how to build houses that summer. He taught us how to do a job right. How to look at a piece of work and know, honestly know, whether it meets the standard or whether you're fooling yourself.

When we started Otto Design + Build, we named it after him. Not as a tribute, but as a promise. Every home we build carries his name, and that means it has to meet his standard. No shortcuts. No "good enough." Done right, or done again.

What We Believe

How we work.

Our word is the foundation

If we say it, we mean it. If we quote it, that's the number. If we promise a timeline, we hit it or we tell you early why we can't. The relationship starts with trust, and we don't take that lightly.

Build it like we're keeping it

Every decision, from framing to finish, gets the same question: would we live with this? If the answer is no, it doesn't go in your house. Or ours.

No hiding behind a desk

We're not project managers who show up for the walkthrough. We're on site, in the dirt, making decisions in real time alongside the people doing the work.

Still here after the keys

Our relationship doesn't end at closing. A year later, five years later, if something needs attention, you have our number. We stand behind the work because our name is on it.
